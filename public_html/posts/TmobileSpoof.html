Spoofing T-Mobile TTL
Right now I am in a rural area where it doesn't make sense to have WiFi when someone's mobile data is unlimited. The carrier detects hotspot usage by inspecting TTL values on outgoing packets — spoofing that value makes your laptop look like the phone itself.
// REBOOTER
TTL=65iptables NATnmcli ipv6=disabledUSB tetheringsysctl --systemDPI bypassmobile hotspotVPN requiredREDIRECT --to-portshop limit overrideTTL=65iptables NATnmcli ipv6=disabledUSB tetheringsysctl --systemDPI bypassmobile hotspotVPN requiredREDIRECT --to-portshop limit override

Why spoof?

The problem: unlimited plans give you unlimited data on the phone itself, but only a small cap of high-speed hotspot data before throttling kicks in. The 10 GB goes out fast. Carriers detect hotspot usage by inspecting the TTL (Time To Live) value on outgoing packets — every router subtracts 1, so when your laptop's traffic passes through the phone it arrives at the tower with TTL 63 instead of 64. That delta is the detection signal.

What I did

Step 1 — Persistent TTL override

BASH█
sudo vim /etc/sysctl.d/99-hotspot.conf

Inside the file:

INI█
net.ipv4.ip_default_ttl=65
net.ipv6.conf.all.hop_limit=65
BASH█
sudo sysctl --system
⚠ IPv6

I also had to disable IPv6 — the tower could tell traffic was not mobile via IPv6 headers.

BASH█
nmcli connection modify "Wired connection 2" ipv6.method "disabled"
nmcli connection up "Wired connection 2"

Step 2 — USB tethering, not Wi-Fi hotspot

  • Stability — wired is more reliable than wireless tethering
  • APN bypass — some carriers trigger hotspot APN detection the moment you activate the Wi-Fi hotspot. USB tethering circumvents that entirely without enabling the hotspot at all
NOTE

When Android enters USB tethering mode, it creates a virtual Ethernet interface on the computer — distinct from the phone's internal rmnet interface. This bypasses standard Wi-Fi hotspot routing entirely.

Step 3 — VPN on the computer, not the phone

If you run the VPN on the phone only: your phone's data is encrypted, but your tethered laptop's traffic bypasses it entirely — it goes naked to the carrier. The VPN defeats Deep Packet Inspection that checks headers for desktop signatures like Mozilla/5.0 X11.

Why this works

The TTL hop logic

Every router subtracts 1 from the TTL. Phone traffic starts at 64 and arrives at 64. Laptop hotspot traffic starts at 64, loses 1 hop through the phone — arrives at 63. That delta is the detection signal.

OriginStart TTLPhone hopCarrier seesResult
Phone64N/A64Safe ✓
Laptop (default)6464 − 163Throttled ✗
Laptop (spoofed)6565 − 164Safe ✓

Why the VPN matters

  • TTL tricks the counter — VPN tricks the inspector
  • Carrier uses DPI to check packet headers for desktop signatures
  • VPN wraps everything in an encrypted blob: carrier sees correct TTL + encrypted garbage = phone app
NOTE

Most carriers don't run DPI unless something flags it — sustained high data usage, unusual UA strings. The TTL fix alone is often enough day-to-day.

CLEANUP

When back on normal internet: rm /etc/sysctl.d/99-hotspot.conf then run sudo sysctl --system.
