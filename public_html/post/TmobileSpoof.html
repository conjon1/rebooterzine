T-mobile TTL spoofing

TESTED ON: T-Mobile · Android Hotspot
$

The Problem

Right now I am in a rural area where it does not make sense to have WiFi whenever someone's mobile data is unlimited. But T-Mobile throttles hotspot traffic by checking the TTL of packets leaving your device.

[INFO] T-Mobile sets hotspot TTL to 63, distinguishing it from device-native traffic at TTL 64.
$

The Fix: TTL spoofing with iptables

We intercept outgoing packets and rewrite the TTL to match what T-Mobile expects from a phone, making your laptop's traffic look native.

bash
# Set outgoing TTL to 65 so after one router hop it reads as 64
iptables -t mangle -A POSTROUTING -j TTL --ttl-set 65
root@reboot:~#curl -s https://ipinfo.io | jq .org
[WARN] Using this may violate your carrier&apos;s ToS. Proceed at your own risk.
